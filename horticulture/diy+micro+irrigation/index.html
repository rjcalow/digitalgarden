Cheap ebay pump

A cheap off-grid solution to micro irrigation. Found on ebay sometimes under upgrade pump or automatic watering system. Has an in built battery and two buttons to set watering interval.

The basic "instructions":

Basic use

  • Pressing both buttons turns on the pump indefinitely.

  • You can set the number of days between watering up to 9. Unsure what 0 setting does.

  • To alter water duration. Hold the time button to set the number of seconds. Can be up-to two minutes.

Cons

  • Short duration of watering.

  • Can only water once a day at most.

  • Lacks filter.

  • Pump loud.

  • Pump not very strong. Struggles with a diy filter attached.

  • How do you know when the battery is low.

Pros

  • Came fully charged.

  • Decent length of pipe.

  • Seems waterproof enough to work in a greenhouse.

Automated irrigation component research

Soil sensors

Warning: Commonplace generic sensors
  • Found throughout ecommerce and within economic kits.
  • Shit. Only work short term.
  • They are resistive, running a current through the soil, slowly destroying itself through electrolysis.

Solution: use a capacitive soil sensor. And furthermore: Use a conformal coating or a plastic bag to separate the pcb from the water. [Source]. [Source].

Pump

Generic micro DC centrifugal pump
  • submersible
  • economical
  • low power
  • all over ebay

Currently testing a unit in a pond. Using a direct hook-up to a 5v solar panel, it pumps water into a small watering can which flows back into the pond creating a water feature.

I have found that:

  • the intake, though small, it can become blocked easily
  • in low light it is a trickle of water
  • in strong direct light you can hear the pump underwater
  • so far I think they are suitable for low power off grid applications that don't need a lot of water all at once.
